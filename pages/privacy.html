Privacy Policy

Effective date: 25 March 2026  |  Last updated: 25 March 2026

Instilligent Limited (NZBN pending) (“Instilligent”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard personal information in accordance with the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020 and the thirteen Information Privacy Principles (IPPs).

This policy covers all Instilligent products and services, including Modular Compliance, Our New Normal, Boss Board, Mastering MOSS, R3 Fleet, Sociamonials, and the instilligent.com website.

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that is reasonably necessary for our functions and activities. This includes:

  • Identity & contact information: Name, email address, phone number, business name, and job title when you register, contact us, or subscribe to a service.
  • Account data: Login credentials (passwords are stored in hashed form only), subscription plan, and billing details.
  • Usage data: Pages visited, features used, session duration, device type, browser, IP address, and referral source — collected via analytics tools.
  • Business data: Information you enter into our products (compliance records, vessel details, invoices, job logs, business listings, etc.).
  • Communications: Emails, support tickets, chat messages, and feedback you send us.
  • Payment information: Processed securely by Stripe. We do not store full credit card numbers.

2. How We Collect Information

We collect information directly from you when you register, use our products, contact us, or subscribe to newsletters. We also collect information automatically through cookies, analytics tools (such as Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity), and server logs. We may receive information from third-party integrations you connect (e.g., Xero, Stripe).

3. Purpose of Collection (IPP 1–4)

We collect and use personal information to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve our products and services
  • Process subscriptions, billing, and payments
  • Send transactional emails (account confirmations, invoices, alerts)
  • Send marketing communications (only with your consent; you can opt out at any time)
  • Respond to support requests and enquiries
  • Analyse usage to improve product features and user experience
  • Meet legal and regulatory obligations
  • Protect against fraud, abuse, and security threats

4. Disclosure of Information (IPP 11)

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information with:

  • Service providers: Cloud hosting (Railway, Cloudflare, AWS), payment processing (Stripe), email delivery (SendGrid/Postmark), analytics (Google Analytics, Microsoft Clarity), and customer support tools.
  • Professional advisors: Lawyers, accountants, and auditors as required.
  • Regulatory authorities: Where required by law or to respond to lawful requests.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, with appropriate confidentiality protections.

5. Data Storage & Security (IPP 5)

Your data is stored on servers located in Australia, the United States, and the European Union via our cloud providers. We implement appropriate technical and organisational safeguards including encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest, access controls, regular backups, and security monitoring. While no system is completely secure, we take reasonable steps to protect your information from unauthorised access, loss, or misuse.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide services. After account closure, we retain data for up to 24 months for legal, tax, and audit purposes, then securely delete or anonymise it. You may request earlier deletion (see Section 9).

7. Cookies & Tracking

Our websites use cookies and similar technologies for essential functionality, analytics, and preference storage. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some product features.

8. Third-Party Links

Our products may contain links to third-party websites or services. We are not responsible for their privacy practices. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing personal information.

9. Your Rights (IPP 6–7)

Under the Privacy Act 2020, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal information (subject to legal retention requirements).
  • Portability: Request your data in a commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Withdraw consent: Opt out of marketing communications at any time.

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details below. We will respond within 20 working days as required by the Privacy Act 2020.

10. Children’s Privacy

Our products are not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child without parental consent, we will take steps to delete it promptly.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via email or prominent notice on our website. The “Last updated” date at the top reflects the most recent revision.
